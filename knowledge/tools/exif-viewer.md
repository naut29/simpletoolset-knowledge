## FAQs

- What is EXIF data?: EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) is metadata embedded in images by cameras. It includes settings like aperture, shutter speed, ISO, GPS location, and timestamp.
- Do all images have EXIF data?: Most photos from cameras and smartphones include EXIF data. Screenshots and graphics typically don't. Some social platforms strip EXIF for privacy.
- Is my image uploaded to a server?: No. All processing happens in your browser. Your images and their metadata never leave your device.
- Can I remove EXIF data for privacy?: This tool only views metadata. To remove EXIF data, you would need a dedicated metadata removal tool before sharing photos.
